Scrappage Policy & De-registration of old Vehicles


Delhi Transport Department recently issued an order stating that diesel vehicles older than 10 years will automatically be deregistered from January 1, 2022, considering the order of the National Green Tribunal (NGT). The department also said that if any ‘end of life (ELV)’ vehicle is found parked in a public space or plying on the road, it will be impounded by the police or transport enforcement unit and sent to the scrapyard. they can avail benefits of the scrappage policy or obtain a no-objection certificate and sell such vehicles outside the capital. Plans are also afoot so people can convert old vehicles to electric, though that will likely take time.

Highlights

  • Diesel vehicles older than 10 years and petrol vehicles more than 15 years will automatically be deregistered from January 1, 2022.
  • If any ‘end of life (ELV)’ vehicle is found parked in a public space or plying on the road, it will be impounded by the police or transport enforcement unit and sent to the scrapyard.
  • The Delhi government has authorised eight scrapyards in NCR and all these authorised vendors have offices in Delhi. If any vehicle owner wants to get their car scrapped, they can call a vendor, and someone will come and pick the vehicle from your doorstep. These yards are also equipped with CCTVs and the owners can see or get a video of their vehicle being scrapped to make sure there is no misuse
  • vehicle owners who opt for this and produce a ‘scrapping certificate’ will get road tax exemption up to 25% rebate on a new personal vehicle. “The owners will also be paid for parts of the vehicle that are in a good condition, as well for iron, which is currently Rs 26 per kg.
  • People whose vehicles are about to reach the age limit can get a no-objection certificate to sell in states where diesel cars older than 10 years and petrol cars older than 15 years can play.

Stakeholders’ Reaction:

  • The Delhi government’s official said people must not go to unauthorised scrapyards as their vehicle “can be used for criminal activities”.
  • Ashish Kundra, Delhi Government’s Transport Commissioner - if a person wants to convert an old vehicle to electric, retrofitting with empanelled kits will be done through agencies approved by the transport department. There are about 10 agencies currently working with the department on this, but it will take time.
  • This is a very good policy for controlling the increasing level of air pollution. Police and government will act but RWAs and housing societies should also take proactive measures and create awareness on the policy.
  • Apart from deregistering these vehicles, the government or NGT should also see that they are also not re-registered and operated in states with a large number of vehicles. Otherwise, there will be no point.


Conclusion
Clarification of the Delhi Government that the end-of-life vehicles could get no objection certificate after its end of life in Delhi so that it could be sold in other states is the welcome news for the owners of “old” vehicles.
