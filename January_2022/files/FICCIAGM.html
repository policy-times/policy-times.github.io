94th Annual General Meeting of FICCI – Key Takeaways

Relevant to all Mahindra Businesses


94th Annual General Meeting of FICCI was organized on 17th-18th December 2021 which was inaugurated by Shri Amit Shah, Union Minister for Home Affairs and Cooperation. FICCI AGM was addressed by Shri Piyush Goyal, Union Minister for Commerce and Industry, Consumer Affairs, Food and Public Distribution; Shri S. Jaishankar, Union Minister for External Affairs; Shri Rajnath Singh, Union Minister for Defense, Government of India. This AGM was also addressed by Shri anurag Jain, Secretary, DPIIT, Shri BB Swain, Secretary, MSME; Shri BVR Subramanyam, Commerce Secretary; Shri R P Gupta, Secretary, Ministry of Environment, Forest, and Climate Change, Government of India; and Shri Arvind Singh, Tourism Secretary, Government of India.

Key Takeaways

  • Shri Amit Shah, Union Home Minister and Chief Guest - Addressing the session 'Vision on New India' - out of the 22 parameters of the economy, India has exceeded in 19 parameters, and this shows that we have come out strong. Both manufacturing and service sector index have reached the pre-COVID levels. “With the announcements of various packages and relief, our inflation is in the range of 4-6 percent as set by the government”. FICCI should identify the potential growth areas, collaborate with the government, and prepare a roadmap for taking India to new heights of prosperity in the next 25 years.
  • Piyush Goyal, Union Commerce Minister - Government is trying to propagate focus on the highest level of integrity, minimum government maximum governance, making life easy for all citizens, and making businesses easy to work in. The fact that the government is willing to learn new ideas, engage with the industry at every level, and work as an enabler, is a sign that the country has good times ahead of us.
  • FTAs - Commerce Ministry is looking to add a 1-2 more FTAs to the existing six. Flavour of today is bilateral trade, and the government is negotiating Free Trade Agreements with multiple countries such as UAE, Australia, United States EU, which will be completed by next year. These will cover many other aspects such as public procurement, intellectual property, social development etc.
  • Global Partnerships - Roughly 300 lines of credit to different countries to promote projects, businesses, and Indian products.
  • Defence - with the current reforms undertaken by the government, the Indian defence and aerospace sector is likely to reach Rs 5 lakh crore by the year 2047 from current 85,000 crore. Government is working to expand the Positive Indigenization List of 208 items to 1,000 items to promote the Indian domestic companies under Atmanirbhar Bharat. Government is committed to increase the budget outlay for Defence Procurement from Indian industry. Come Make in India, come make for India, come make for the world. Corporatization of OFB is probably the biggest reform in Defence Production Sector since independence.
  • Policy Reforms & Ease of Doing Business - Shri Anurag Jain, Secretary, DPIIT said that the government has focused on holistic reforms in all the sectors and remarkable efforts of current government have led to easing of several compliances across sectors and States. Three most important projects which they are working on with high priority are PM Gatishakti Project, Ease of Living, and Start-up India. To reduce the compliance burden, Shri Jain said that the work has been done in two phases, where about 25,000 compliances have been reduced jointly by the efforts of the States and the Central government. The government is working towards simplification, removal of redundant laws, digitization, and decriminalization to ease the business processes. Several initiatives have been taken in the area of drone and geospatial maps. Industry should also make use of the National Single Window portal where 19 Departments and 10 States are already connected and provide feedback to DPIIT for further improvements.
  • Environment & Climate Change - Government will mandate a single national portal for registration for Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) under Plastic Waste Management Rules, 2016. Government focus is on reducing the number of approvals and thereby improving the compliance requirements and focusing on the monitoring of compliance. Government will incentivize higher recovery and recycling and provide a free market mechanism for efficient solutions to the problem of plastic waste management. There will be a world-class portal for a single national-level registration for plastic waste management. This will be a comprehensive portal that will take everything to do with plastic waste management regulations and EPR and will also take care of the market-based certification mechanism for EPR. Government is conscious of the fact that increasing micron thickness will increase the GHG emissions and probably this is the only situation where measures for plastic waste management and reduction in GHG emissions are not going hand in hand, but the ministry is open to solution for alternatives going forward. India will have a national carbon market in the future. There should be technology-neutral and fuel neutral options for decarbonization of the transport sector.
  • MSME – Central Government is working with State Governments to increase the number of Micro and Small Enterprises Facilitation Councils and the number is increasing gradually to address the incremental cases. MSME Ministry is in the process to announce a scheme on Digital Literacy for Micro Units to expand the horizon of Digitization in MSME Sector. Government has come out with the 'Self-Reliant Fund' which is created on the model of mother & daughter fund to support progressive medium enterprises through equity intervention with an expectation of total provision of Rs 50,000 Crore through daughter funds. Government is closely working with the Department of Commerce on National Resource Database System to develop the Global Market Intelligence System (GMIS), to provide data on market intelligence for MSMEs. It is important to identify Indian Products having potential in the global market, pinpointing the world-class element in it, further researching to find ways to improve the same and start interventions.
  • Trade & Commerce - Shri BVR Subrahmanyam, Commerce Secretary, Govt of India pointed out that the Indian government is working on Future Ready Commerce. This will work on multiple things. First and foremost, there will be a separate export promotion wing with a footprint in all Indian missions abroad and in every state of India. A Bi-lateral and Multilateral engagement department will be constituted under the trade negotiation wing. A market intelligence will also be set up across the states of India which will be a great boon for the industry in boosting their export. In addition, the Ministry is working on a Brand India strategy under an overarching objective of trade promotion across the globe.
  • Tourism - Focus on promoting wellness tourism in a safe and healthy destination for international travellers. Industry can help the sector by improving the skill sets, services and infrastructure that will give the traveller a world class experience.
