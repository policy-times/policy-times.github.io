Editor's Desk

Amid the celebrations of its 75thIndependence Day, India witnessed a number of developments which would go a long way in changing the global perception of India.
Right from Neeraj Chopra’s gold medal, the first in athletics for India in the Olympics, to the withdrawal of the contentious retrospective taxation and the announcement of a National Hydrogen Mission by the Prime Minister from the ramparts of the Red Fort, all these events would remain landmark developments for the country.  

The positive developments of August followed several key policy decisions and legislative actions taken in the past two months which are likely to go a long way in changing the country’s economic and industrial landscape.

National Policies

Ministry of Civil Aviation 

Drone Rules, 2021

Relevant to MLL and Agri Business

August 26, 2021 

  • ‘Drone' rules to unleash new age animal spirits, generate employment, give economic benefits. Liberalised ‘Drone Rules, 2021’ will not only allow a smooth take-off of unmanned aircraft systems but also generate employment, accumulate economic benefits and unleash new services across the country.

  • The Centre replaced the UAS Rules with the liberalised ‘Drone Rules, which are envisioned on a premise of trust, self-certification and non-intrusive monitoring.

Ministry of Commerce & Industry  

RoDTEP Scheme Guidelines and Rates (Remission of Duties and Taxes on Exported Products) 

Relevant for IO

August 17, 2021

  • India’s attempts to reach the $400 billion export target in FY22 received a major boost from 'Refund of Duties and Taxes on Exported Products' scheme. 

  • The scheme is based on globally accepted principle that taxes and duties should not be exported. The scheme provides refunds to exporters on taxes and levies borne on exported products. The scheme notified this month has replaced the Merchandise Exports from India Scheme (MEIS).

Prime Minister’s Office 

Prime Minister launches Vehicle Scrappage Policy 

Relevant for CERO

August 13, 2021 

  • In a much awaited development, Prime Minister Narendra Modi on August 13 launched the Vehicle Scrappage Policy in a bid to phase out old and environmentally-unfit vehicles.

  • Noting that the move is a significant milestone, PM Modi tweeted: “I would request our youth & start-ups to join this programme. Vehicle scrapping will help phase out unfit & polluting vehicles in an environment friendly manner. Our aim is to create a viable #circulareconomy & bring value for all stakeholders while being environmentally responsible."
