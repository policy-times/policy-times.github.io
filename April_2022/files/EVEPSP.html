West Bengal: Electric Vehicle Ecosystem Policy 2022


In order to develop West Bengal as a leading state in manufacturing of Electric Vehicles and develop the allied ecosystem that facilitates development of EV ecosystem and deployment of Electric Vehicles in the state, the West Bengal Government has come up with the ‘Draft Electric Vehicle Ecosystem Policy 2022’. The focus is to create a comprehensive EV ecosystem in the State by attracting investments in the sector.

Objectives

  • Make West Bengal the preferred destination for Electric Vehicle and component manufacturing.
  • Create a pool of skilled workforce for the industry and create new jobs
  • Promote research and development, innovation, and skill development across the EV ecosystem.
  • Encourage cutting-edge research and innovation in Electric vehicles, battery technologies and other emerging technologies
  • Promote Recycle and Swapping of Batteries
  • Create robust infrastructure for electric vehicles including adequate power supply and network of charging points with favourable power tariff.
  • Endorse a shift in mobility by increasing adoption of electric vehicles
  • Generate demand for EV and battery storage solutions by providing EV adoption incentives and supply side incentives for manufacturing
  • Support creation of EV charging Infrastructure and create market for commercially viable EV Charging business

Highlights

  • Incentives and concessions will be offered by the Government of West Bengal to promote investments in Electric Vehicle Manufacturing, EV Battery Manufacturing, EV Charging Infrastructure equipment manufacturing including installation and setting up of the same, and Equipment Manufacturing Enterprises.
  • The incentives and benefits will be applicable for a period of five years.
  • The Government of West Bengal will implement policy interventions, both fiscal and non-fiscal.
  • A wide array of incentives including investment subsidies, tax rebates, interest subvention, etc. would be provided for EV manufacturers setting up in West Bengal.
  • The Government would also ensure availability of land in clusters, with readymade facilities for power, water sewage and testing facilities along with dedicated testing tracks.
  • The incentives will also extend to preferential treatments, creation of EV Research Hubs, and creation of Center of Excellence etc.
  • In order to further provide a conducive environment for EV manufacturers, the Government will also provide assistance across various procedures including Patent Filing, and Skill Development etc.
  • The Government of West Bengal will formulate incentives and benefits for encouraging Research and Development in the EV space.
  • Government will also offer additional incentive to encourage start-ups and research hubs working towards manufacture of modular design lithium-ion batteries.
Following incentives would be offered in the supply side and demand side:
Supply side: Fiscal benefits
  • Investment promotion subsidy
  • State GST reimbursement
  • Electricity tax exemption
  • power tariff discount
  • Exemption from stamp duty
  • Interest subvention
  • Employment incentive
  • Special benefits for EV battery manufacturing
  • Subsidy for effluent treatment plant
Supply Side: Non-fiscal Benefits
  • Preferential market access in government procurement,
  • Exemption of state/municipal tax payment,
  • Capital subsidy for service providers including charging stations,
  • Battery switching / swapping stations etc.
  • Providing transition support for manufacturers
  • Establishment of EV manufacturing parks
  • Creation of Vendor Ecosystem assistance in Patent Filing
  • Reimbursement of Quality Certification costs
  • Cleaner Production cost reimbursement
  • 100% Skill Development Assistance through state agencies
  • Development of battery recycling landscape
Supply side: Other benefits
  • Development of EV research hub
  • centre of excellence
  • Start-up funds and incubation centres
  • Up-skilling, training and job creation
  • Formation of an apex Steering Committee to guide and monitor the implementation of the policy
Demand Side Benefits
  • Purchase incentive to the consumers including early bird incentives over and above purchase incentives
  • Concessional interest rate for buyers
  • Road Tax and Registration charges exemption
  • Parking charge exemption,
  • Use of EV in e-commerce industry
  • Tech-enabled and web-based incentive delivery portal for policy implementation
  • Early disbursement of subsidy/incentives
  • Creation of EV Zones
  • Development of EV charging infrastructure,
  • Exemption from Stamp Duty on land for EV charging infrastructure stations.
Health and Family Welfare
  • Rs 1,009 crore has been allocated for Primary Health Centres (PHCs).
  • Rs 2,462 crore has been allocated towards the National Health Mission.
Transport
  • Rs 2,173 crore has been allocated for capital outlay on roads and bridges.
Irrigation and Flood Control
  • Rs 2,366 crore has been allocated towards dam works.
  • Rs 1,256 crore has been allocated towards resettlement and rehabilitation.

Conclusion

As Electric vehicles are the future of the transport ecosystem; this initiative of the West Bengal Government could be a game changer in making the state future ready and making a hub of Electric Vehicle Manufacturing. This policy appropriately focuses on creating a robust Electric Vehicle Ecosystem in the state. Effective implementation would be key to for desired outcome of the policy.
