Karnataka Budget 2022-23: Highlights and Key Announcements


The Chief Minister of Karnataka, Mr. Basavaraj Bommai, presented the Budget for the state for the financial year 2022-23 on March 4, 2022.

Highlights

Gross State Domestic Product (GSDP) Rs 18,85,750 crore (at current Prices) in 2022-23(estimated) Increase of 9.6 % as per the revised estimate of 2021-22
Total Expenditure Rs 2,51,541 crore increase of 5%
Revenue Deficit Rs 24,353 crore 0.68% of the GSDP
Fiscal Deficit Rs 14,699 crore 0.78% of GSDP
Education
  • Rs 17,184 crore has been allocated towards elementary education.
  • Rs 960 crore has been allocated for Mid-Day Meal Programme.
Agriculture
  • Raitha Shakti scheme to be launched to encourage the use of farm machinery by giving diesel subsidy of Rs 250 per acre.
  • Yashashwini scheme to be re-introduced to provide medical facilities to farmer families of rural areas.
  • Ksheera Samruddhi Sahakara Bank to be established for giving loans to milk producers. Existing veterinary clinics to be strengthened and 100 new such clinics to be commended in phases.
  • Rs 2,000 crore has been allocated towards Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Yojana.
  • Rs 927 crore has been allocated towards crop insurance.
Industry
  • Karnataka Special Investment Region Act to be formulated for the establishment, operation, control, and management of mega industrial regions.
  • Initially, Tumakuru and Dharwad districts to be notified as Special Investment Region.
Rural Development
  • Rs 1,283 crore has been allocated towards MGNREGS.
Health and Family Welfare
  • Namma Clinics to be established in major cities of Karnataka and in all wards of Bengaluru.
  • These clinics to provide testing facilities for non-communicable diseases.

Urban Development

Energy
