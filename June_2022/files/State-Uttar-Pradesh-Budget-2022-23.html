Uttar Pradesh Budget 2022-23: Highlights and Key Announcements


Uttar Pradesh Finance Minister, Mr. Suresh Khanna, presented the Budget for the state for the financial year 2022-23 on May 26, 2022.

Budget Highlights

  • Gross State Domestic Product (GSDP) – GSDP of Uttar Pradesh for 2022-23 (at current prices) is projected to be Rs 20,48,234 crore, a growth of 17.1% over the revised estimate of GSDP for 2021-22 (Rs 17,49,469 crore). In 2021-22, GSDP is estimated to contract by 9.8 % over the previous year (at current prices).
  • Expenditure (excluding debt repayment) - In 2022-23, it is estimated to be Rs 5,82,956 crore, a 28% increase over the revised estimates of 2021-22 (Rs 4,55,809 crore). In addition, debt of Rs 32,563 crore will be repaid by the state in 2022-23. In 2021-22, expenditure (excluding debt repayment) is estimated to be 11% lower than the budget estimate.
  • Receipts (excluding borrowings) - For 2022-23, it is estimated to be Rs 5,01,778 crore, an increase of 32% over the revised estimates of 2021-22 (Rs 3,81,063 crore). In 2021-22, receipts (excluding borrowings) are estimated to fall short of the budget estimate by Rs 39,609 crore (a decrease of 9%).
  • Fiscal deficit - For 2022-23 it is targeted at Rs 81,178 crore (3.96% of GSDP). In 2021-22, as per the revised estimates, fiscal deficit is expected to be 4.27% of GSDP, higher than the budget estimate of 4.17% of GSDP.
  • Revenue surplus For 2022-23, it is estimated to be Rs 43,124 crore (2.11% of the GSDP). In 2021-22, the state is estimated to observe a revenue surplus of 1.26% of GSDP, higher than the budget estimate of 1.07% of GSDP.

Education
  • Rs 15,169 crore has been allocated towards primary education under Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan.
  • In the year 2022-2023, a target of 2 crore student enrolment has been set in the council schools under "School Chalo Abhiyan".
  • A provision of Rs. 18,670 crore 72 lakh has been proposed for the Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan.
  • Rs. 370 crore proposed for distribution of free uniforms, sweaters, school bags and shoe-stockings for the use of students studying from classes 1 to 8.
  • Rs 98 crore 38 lakh proposed for functioning of Sainik Schools.
  • To make Sanskrit education vocational, the syllabus of NCERT has been implemented by including modern subjects. Rs. 324 crore 41 lakh proposed for grants to Sanskrit schools.
  • Under the Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan, Rs. 836 crore 80 lakh proposed in the budget for the financial year 2022-23 for various construction and infrastructure development works.
  • Rs. 10 crores proposed for the establishment of smart classes for the government colleges in the state.
  • Rs. 200 crores proposed for completion of buildings being constructed in state government colleges.
  • Construction work is in progress for the establishment of 75 new government colleges in the state. (15)
  • In order to provide free online Sanskrit training to the students, establishment of online Sanskrit training centre at Sampurnanand Sanskrit Vishwavidyalaya, Varanasi has been proposed. Students will be provided training in employment courses through it. Rs. 1 crore 16 lakh proposed for setting up of training centre.
  • In order to make the state a one trillion dollar economy, the state government has planned to provide more employment to the students and manpower for emerging technology-based industries, in the session 2022-23. Under "New Age Trades", students will be given training in four courses - Data Science and machine learning, internet of things, cyber security, and drone technology.
  • Development of 04 ITI as model ITI of the state proposed.
  • A total of 31 out of the new ITI set up in different districts of the state, will be run on the PPP model.
  • Target to provide training by registering about 2 lakh youth in the financial year 2022-23 under Uttar Pradesh Skill Development Mission.
Agriculture
  • A provision of Rs. 97.42 crore for land and water management scheme for the coastal area of Nirmal Ganga under Namami Gange Cleanliness Campaign.
  • A provision of Rs. 10 crores have been proposed for Agriculture College, Lakhimpur Campus and other construction works under Chandrashekhar Azad Agricultural and Technological University, Kanpur.
  • A provision of Rs. 8.58 crore has been proposed for various construction works of Banda Agricultural and Technological University.
  • A provision of Rs. 60 crores have been proposed for the scheme for strengthening and reviving the existing milk unions.
  • A provision of Rs. 8 crores have been proposed for the construction of a new dairy plant of 3000 litres per day capacity in district Mathura.
  • Provision of Rs. 79 crore 82 lakh for the under construction Green Field Dairy Plant in Varanasi and Meerut districts has been made.
  • Mukhyamantri Matsya Sampada Yojana is being started to make fish farmers financially self-sufficient, for which a provision of Rs. 2 crores have been made.
  • Nishadraj Boat Subsidy Scheme is proposed to help fishing lease holders and fishermen of Gram Sabha ponds to purchase boats, for which a provision of Rs. 2 crore has been proposed
Special Development Packages
  • To implement development programs at the constituency level, in line with local needs and preferences, a Special Development Package Fund is being established.
  • Every Member of Legislative Assembly will have two crore rupees at their disposal to implement development programs.
Rural Development
  • A provision of Rs. 7000 crores has been proposed for Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (Gramin).
  • A provision of Rs. 508 crore 63 lakh has been proposed for the scheme for the year 2022-2023 under the Mukhyamantri Awas Yojana-Gramin.
  • The state government will be doing selection of 15,463 ponds and 5,882 sports grounds in the financial year 2022-2023 under MNREGA scheme as promised in the Lok Kalyan Sankalp Patra-2022.
  • Under the Shyama Prasad Mukherji Rurban Mission, a provision of Rs. 155.60 crore has been proposed in the budget for the financial year 2022- 2023.
  • Under the Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana, a provision of Rs. 7373.71 crore has been proposed for the scheme in 2022-2023.
  • Under the National Rural Livelihood Mission for the financial year 2022- 2023, a budget arrangement of Rs. 3155.10 crore has been proposed for various components.
Infrastructure and Industrial Development
  • Plan for attracting investment of Rs. 10 lakh crores in next 10 years.
  • Rs. 100 crores proposed the implementation of 1st phase of Atal Industrial Infrastructure Mission.
  • Rs. 897 crores proposed for multi-modal connectivity projects under PM Gati Shakti Yojana.
  • Rs. 300 crores proposed for capital projects on optical fibre cable network.
  • Rs. 200 crores proposed for capital projects to encourage digitisation in the state.
  • Rs. 500 crores proposed in the 1st phase for the development of industrial corridor beside all green field expressways.
  • Rs. 694 crore 34 lakh proposed for 594 km. long 6-lane Ganga Expressway from Meerut to Prayagraj. Construction of airstrip for emergency landing of air force planes in Shahjahanpur under this expressway project
Sugarcane Development and Sugar Industry
  • A total of Rs. 1000 crore has been proposed for payment of sugarcane price to the remaining sugarcane farmers.
  • A provision of Rs. 380 crore has been proposed for the renovation and modernisation of sugar mills and the establishment of new sugar mills in the state.
Medical Education
  • Work in progress to set up at least one medical college in every district. At present, 65 medical colleges under operation in state, 45 districts have at least 01 medical college and medical colleges are under construction in 14 districts.
  • Medical colleges to be set up in 16 un-served districts.
  • Under Lok Kalyan Sankalp Patra-2022, Rs. 500 crores provided for increasing MBBS and PG seats in state.
  • An amount of Rs. 50 crores proposed for running of Autonomous State Medical Colleges.
  • Rs. 25 crores for setting up Nursing College.
  • Rs. 145 crores provided for establishing Atal Bihari Vajpayee Medical University in Lucknow.
  • Rs. 2100 crores provided for setting up new medical colleges in 14 districts.
  • Rs. 50 crores earmarked for Pt. Deen Dayal Upadhyay State Employees Cashless Medical Scheme.
  • Rs. 100 crores provided for treatment of chronic diseases among economically poor people in the state.
Urban Development
  • Rs. 1353.93 crore has been proposed for Swachchha Bharat Mission (Urban).
  • There is a plan by the central government to provide every household with the facility of pure drinking water through taps. Assistance under Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation for the said scheme, Rs. 2000 crore under AMRUT 2.0 and Rs. 2200 crore for the already ongoing AMRUT scheme have been proposed.
  • Rs. 10,127.61 crores have been proposed for the Housing for All (Urban) Scheme under the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana.
  • A total of Rs. 2,000 crores for 10 selected cities under Central Smart City and Rs. 210 crores for 7 selected cities under State Smart City have been proposed. Under the Smart City Mission Scheme, out of the first 20 cities of the country, 5 cities are from Uttar Pradesh.
  • Rs. 550 crores have been proposed for the development of infrastructure facilities in newly created, elevated, and expanded municipal bodies in the state.
  • Rs. 100 crores have been proposed for the preparation of Mahakumbh Mela-2025 Prayagraj.
  • Rs. 200 crores have been proposed for Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay Adarsh Nagar Panchayat Yojana.
  • Rs. 215 crores have been proposed for the Chief Minister's Urban Underdeveloped and Slums Scheme.
  • Rs. 100 crores has been proposed for Kanha Gaushala and Destitute Animal Shelter Scheme.
IT & Electronics
  • Under the Uttar Pradesh Data Centre Policy-2021, a target of developing 250 MW data centre industry by establishing 03 data centre parks with an investment of Rs. 20,000 crores.
Energy and Additional Energy
  • Uninterrupted power supply will be ensured in rural areas from 7.00 pm to 6.00 am during summer, for which a provision of Rs. 1000 crore has been proposed.
  • A 50% discount will be provided on electricity bills of private tube well consumers from 1st January 2022.
  • An ambitious Rs. 31,000 crore ‘Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme’ is being started in association with the Central government. An arrangement of Rs. 5530 crores made under budget. The scheme will be completed within the next three years.
  • Under ‘Babu Ji Kalyan Singh Gram Unnati Yojana’, solar streetlights will be installed on the roads of all the villages of the state. A provision of Rs. 22 crore 50 lakh has been made for the same.
Tourism
  • Rs. 3 core 50 lakh proposed for Uttar Pradesh Vindhya Dham Teerth Vikas Parishad and Rs. 3 core 50 lakh for Uttar Pradesh Chitrakoot Dham pilgrimage development.
  • Rs. 250 crores proposed for Mukhyamantri Paryatan Sthal Vikas Yojna.
  • Rs. 100 crores proposed for the development and beautification of tourism facilities in Varanasi.
  • Rs. 100 crores proposed for the development and beautification of tourism facilities in Ayodhya.
Handloom and Textiles
  • A provision of Rs. 8 crores have been proposed for the operation of the Jhalkari Bai Kori Handloom and Power Loom Special Component Scheme with the aim of promoting self-employment to the weavers of scheduled castes.
  • Electricity is being provided to the power loom weavers of the state at concessional rates. A provision of Rs. 250 crores have been made for the same.
  • To eliminate the dependence of handloom weavers on traditional energy sources, a budget arrangement of Rs. 10 crores has been proposed under the 'Mukhyamantri Bunkar Saur Yojana' to provide solar inverters to power loom weavers and handloom weavers.
Irrigation and Flood Control
  • Rs. 2751 crore has been proposed for flood control and drainage.
  • Rs. 356 crores have been proposed for the projects of construction/upgrade/strengthening of embankments.
  • Rs. 1328 crore has been proposed for river improvement and anti-erosion schemes.
  • Rs. 144 crores has been proposed for the projects of drainage (NABARD funded).
  • Rs. 800 crore has been proposed for providing free water to farmers from canals and government tube wells.
  • Under the LTIF scheme, in the financial year 2022-23, a budget of Rs. 600 crores for the Madhya Ganga Canal Project, Rs. 310 crores for the Saryu Canal Project and Rs. 20 crores for the Arjun Sahayak Project have been proposed.
  • Rs. 423 crores for the project of construction of 2,100 new state tube wells (NABARD funded) and Rs. 150 crores for the project of modernisation of 6,600 state tube wells (NABARD funded) have been proposed.
  • Rs. 130 crores have been proposed for the project of reconstruction of 569 failed state tube wells.

Reactions

  • The government should focus more on higher education, which is crucial in shaping students’ careers. YS Rajasekhar Reddy, during his tenure as CM, established universities for each district, which are now lacking in basic amenities and infrastructure.
  • The government should have allotted Rs 1,000-2,000 crore more for higher education.
KSS Prasad, Secretary, United Teachers Federation (UTF)
  • The government should at least allocate 30% of the total funds to the sector
Suresh Rayudu Chitturi, Chairman, CII AP & Telangana,
  • Launching a mission-based development strategy in the budget would certainly have positive impact in delivering the results and implementation of various governmental programmes
  • Government’s moves to build Greenfield Airport between Vijayawada and Kakinada besides upgrading existing airport in Vijayawada and establishment of LNG terminal at Kakinada are good moves for the state.
  • Setting up of solar power parks in Guntur and Anantapur districts and encouraging the usage of solar pump sets for farmers are welcome steps.
