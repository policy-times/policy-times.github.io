Editor's Desk
The month of May was marked by concern of rising inflation. Government circles have been agog with potential fall outs. After months of dragging their feet, the government has suddenly got into a firefighting mode, in a desperate attempt to arrest surging prices. As a part of these efforts, the Reserve Bank of India called an off-cycle policy meeting and increased the cash reserve ratio by 50 basis points to 4.5% and the repo rate by 40 basis points to 4.4%, the first-rate hike after August 2018. The government followed this with a ban on wheat exports, resulting in vocal protests from across the globe. Later, it announced an excise duty cut on diesel and petrol and a Rs.200 subsidy on cooking gas cylinders for 12 months to prevent fuel prices from further scorching consumers. The government has also allowed duty-free imports of edible oils and restrained sugar exports to reduce the pressure on food prices.

A recent report titled ‘The State of Inequality in India’ by the Institute for Competitiveness, brought out in response to the request of the Economic Advisory Council to the Prime Minister (EAC-PM), has made a few surprising recommendations. This includes raising minimum income and providing universal basic income to reduce the growing income gap and the introduction of an urban version of the demand-based Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act to rehabilitate surplus labour.

The Delhi Government announced its start-up policy at the beginning of this month to encourage the creation of a robust start-up ecosystem and to attract start-ups. Delhi too wants to be seen as an important Start-up hub of India.

On the multilateral front, the announcement of a thirteen-member international group- Indo-Pacific Economic Framework (IPEF) for Prosperity with India being one of the members of the group, created quite a stir.

One of the major attractions this month was the resurgence of the World Economic Summit at Davos in a physical form after two years. India participated in this prestigious global event with a strong delegation from the Government and the industry including various State Governments.

On data governance and management, two major developments occured i.e. the publication of a draft National Data Governance Framework policy by MEITY and the creation of a National Data and Analytics Platform by NITI Aayog. There have been some developments in the clean environment policy which includes amendments in National Biofuel policy and a draft notification for e-waste management.

Supreme Court’s ruling on center-state relations on the recommendations of GST Council could have far reaching effects. The judgment may change the landscape of those provisions under GST which are subject to judicial review. The court has categorically stated that the GST Council recommendations have only persuasive value, there will be a pragmatic approach to the provisions which are subject to judicial review by way of challenge to the constitutionality of such provisions based on GST Council recommendations.

The announcement of budget 2022-23 in Uttar Pradesh by the new government which enunciated a revenue surplus, was another interesting development during the month.
