Draft National Data Governance Framework Policy

Relevant to Tech M


Recently, the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) released a revised Draft National Data Governance Framework Policy.

Context
After a strong criticism of the previous ‘Draft India Data Accessibility and Use Policy, 2022’, which was opened for public consultation in February this year and had aims to “radically transform India’s ability to harness public sector data”, the ministry has come up with this new draft of National Data Governance Framework Policy (NDGFP). The earlier policy was proposed to permit the licensing and sale of public data by the government to the private sector.

Key Objectives

  1. To transform and modernize Government’s data collection and management processes and systems through standardised guidelines, rules and standards for the collection, processing, storage, access, and use of Government data
  2. To enable and catalyse vibrant Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Data led research and Start-up ecosystem
  3. To accelerate Digital Governance
  4. To have standardized data management and security standards across the Government
  5. To accelerate creation of common standard based public digital platforms while ensuring privacy, safety, and trust
  6. To have standard APls and other tech standards for Whole of Government Data management and access.
  7. To promote transparency, accountability, and ownership in non-personal data and Datasets access. For purposes of safety and trust, any non-personal data sharing by any entity can be only via platforms designated and authorised by IDMO.
  8. To build a platform that will allow Dataset requests to be received and processed.
  9. To build Digital Government goals and capacity, knowledge and competency in Government departments and entities.
  10. To set quality standards and promote expansion of India Datasets program and overall non-personal Datasets Ecosystem.
  11. To ensure greater citizen awareness, participation, and engagement.

Applicability

  • All central Government departments and entities and rules and standards prescribed will cover all data collected and being managed by any central Government entity.
  • All non-personal datasets and data and platform, rules, standards governing its access and use by researchers and Start-ups.
  • With the implementation of NDGFP, the Government plans to create a one-stop Indian Datasets Platform consisting of anonymized non-personal datasets from Indian citizens or those in India
  • This platform shall process requests and provide access to the non-personal anonymized datasets to Indian researchers and start-ups.
  • State Governments shall be encouraged to adopt the provisions of the Policy and rules, standards, and protocols as applicable
  • Although NDGFP does not apply to the private players per se, they can voluntarily contribute their datasets to the data repository made under NDGFP.

Key Provisions

  • An “India Data Management Office (IDMO)” shall be set up under the Digital India Corporation (“DIC”) under MeitY and shall be responsible for framing, managing and periodically reviewing and revising the Policy.
  • The IDMO will be responsible for the implementation and enforcement of the NDGFP and rules and standards issued from time
  • The framework encapsulates three pronged functions of the IDMO:

  1. Creation of Indian Datasets Platform
    • The IDMO shall be responsible for developing rules, standards, and guidelines under this Policy that shall be published periodically.
    • The IDMO shall formulate all data/datasets/metadata rules, standards, and guidelines in consultation with Ministries, State Governments, and industry.
    • The IDMO shall conduct at least 2 semi-annual consultations and report carding for this purpose with representation from State Governments and industry.
    • IDMO will design and manage the India Datasets platform that will process requests and provide access to the non-personal and/or anonymized datasets to Indian researchers and Start-ups.
    • Every Ministry/Department shall have Data Management Units (“DMUs”) headed by a designated CDO who shall work closely with the IDMO for ensuring implementation of the Policy.
  2. Safeguarding Data Usage
    • IDMO shall notify protocols for sharing of non-personal datasets while ensuring privacy, security, and trust. Rules to provide data on priority or exclusively to Indian/ India based requesting entities shall also be developed.
    • For datasets other than those available on the Open Data Portal, data requests shall be considered based on their genuineness and validity by the IDMO. It shall also decide whether requesting entities can be allowed access to complete databases or combinations thereof for their use cases.
    • IDMO shall publish disclosure norms for data collected/ stored/ shared and accessed over a certain threshold.
    • IDMO shall define principles for ethical and fair use of data beyond the Government ecosystem.
    • IDMO may charge user charges/ fees for its maintenance/ fees
    • The IDMO may ensure that data usage rights along with permissioned purposes to be with the Data Principal.
    • IDMO will formulate disclosure norms for data collected/ stored/ shared and accessed over a certain threshold.
    • The IDMO shall define the principles for ethical and fair use of data shared beyond the government ecosystem
    • The IDMO shall institute a mechanism for citizens to request datasets, register grievances and establish responsibility of DMUs under the IDMO to respond in a timely manner, to facilitate transparent and accountable data sharing ecosystem.
    • Every Ministry/Department shall have Data Management Units (“DMUs”) headed by a designated CDO who shall work closely with the IDMO for ensuring implementation of the Policy.
    • IDMO will set and publish data anonymization standards and rules to ensure information privacy is maintained.
  3. Capacity Building of Government Bodies
    • The IDMO shall support holistic and comprehensive capacity building initiatives for officials in all government agencies to build data and digital literacy, knowledge, and skills.
    • The IDMO shall also assist in setting up DMUs in Ministries and Departments to create dedicated capacity for data management.
    • The IDMO shall ensure adequate awareness building by sharing SOPs, FAQs, Operating Manuals and shall also ensure appropriate branding for quick adoption of the policy.

Concerns

  • Specific policies governing standards for data anonymization, rules for conditions of access to such data to private players, rules safeguarding processing and fair and ethical usage of such data by private entities shall be key to ensuring a safe and transparent data regime.
  • Concerns like data privacy, security, intellectual property, and data monopoly issues seem open-ended at the moment.
  • A technical threshold for data anonymization should be specified.
  • As NDGFP has done away with the 'monetization' provisions of the previous Draft India Data Accessibility and Use Policy, it is to be seen if the current framework be sufficient to ensure a non-monopolistic data market fair for all market players.
  • It is necessary to ensure a fair data market for all and stronger regulations to prevent misuse of non-personal data.
  • There exists huge challenge to implement this policy. Streamlining data sets across Government entities and ending data silos is a humongous task for the Government that will require transformational capacity building including changing the behaviour of Government functionaries.

Conclusion

 It is intended that NDGFP would accelerate data driven digital governance by providing the scope for informed decision making, enhanced progrmme evaluation and more efficient service delivery. This policy is also helpful in catalyzing data and AI start-ups by providing anonymized and non-personal data sets to fuel research and innovation. Streamlining data sets across government entities is one of the striking features of this policy. But the smooth implementation is the key to get all these benefits.
