Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs

Central Government Scheme for running of nearly 10,500 buses in 108 cities

Relevant to M&M – Auto/MTBD


  • A scheme is likely to be rolled out to provide financial assistance for running of nearly 10,500 busses in 108 cities.
  • Cities with population in the range of 5-40 lakh will qualify for the scheme which will be in operation for eight years.

Context
In her budget speech on February 1, 2021, Union finance minister Nirmala Sitharaman had said the scheme will provide Rs 18,000 crore financial support for five years to augment public transport system across India. She said that the scheme will facilitate deployment of innovative PPP model to enable private sector players to finance, acquire, operate and maintain over 20,000.

Key Points

  • An inter-ministerial panel mandated for examining the financial aspect has given the go ahead for Rs 18,000 Crore scheme and the housing and urban affairs ministry will now circulate proposal for Cabinet’s approval.
  • Each of the eligible cities would be able to bring around 100 city busses under this scheme. The finance ministry is of the opinion that the number of buses for cities can be increased, if there is a need.
  • The Centre’s funding assistance will be under the operational expenditure model in which the ministry will give the share of the payment that for running busses that cities will make to private operators for running busses sometimes on the line of cluster bus services.
  • To be implemented under public-private partnership (PPP) model, states will invite bids from private bus operators. The private operators will quote the price for the contracts for fixed period. The operator will get paid on per kilometre basis.
  • Sources said the Centre would pay around 25% of the contractor value in case of cities with 20-40 lakh populations. For cities with less population would get nearly 35% of the contract value from the central government. Cities which would start the bus service for the first time would get an additional payment of 5% and those opting for CNG buses will get 2% more assistance as well.

Conclusion

 Although the scheme is likely to be launched after 15 months of its announcement in the Union Budget 2021-22, this has the potential to enhance urban transport infrastructure. We need to wait till the announcement of the scheme in its final shape. Once announced, implement of the scheme in the time bound manner is necessary to get the desired outcome.
