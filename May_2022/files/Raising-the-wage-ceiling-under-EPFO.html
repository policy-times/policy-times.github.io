Ministry of Labour & Employment

Raising the wage ceiling under EPFO to Rs 21,000 – Report by Ad-hoc panel

Relevant to HR Team of all Mahindra Group Companies


The ad-hoc committee backed the proposed increase in wage ceiling under the Employees' Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO) to Rs 21,000 a month. This committee has said the government can implement the increase from a later date after deliberations.

EPFO
The EPFO is committed to extending social security benefits in the form of Provident, Pension and Insurance Funds to the organised workforce of the country, covered under the aegis of Employees' Provident Funds & Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952.

Any company with over 20 employees must register with the EPFO and the EPF scheme is compulsory for all employees earning less than Rs 15,000.

A person covered under EPF gets benefits under three schemes — provident fund, pension, and insurance — run by the EPFO. In the defined contribution-based scheme, employees contribute 12% of their wages while their employers contribute 13% of the employees’ wage into the scheme. Of employers’ 13% contribution, 3.67% goes into the EPF account, 8.33% in EPS and 0.5% each in EDLI scheme and in the administrative account.

Last Amendment
The last such amendment related to the EPFO salary limit was made in 2014. At that time, the government had raised the provident fund salary limit from Rs 6,500 to Rs 15,000.

The suggestion made by the retirement fund body holds significance as the salary increase will align the ceiling with the ESI (Employee State Insurance) scheme. The limit for this social security scheme is also Rs 21,000

Benefits to the Employees

Once the proposal is implemented, it will bring an estimated 7.5 million additional workers within the fold of the scheme and also bring a relief for the exchequer as the central government at present pays about Rs 6,750 crore annually to the Employees' Pension Scheme.

The increase in the limit to Rs 21,000 will bring more workers under the retirement scheme and align the ceiling with the other social security scheme Employees' State Insurance Corporation (ESIC) where the limit is Rs 21,000, the report added.
