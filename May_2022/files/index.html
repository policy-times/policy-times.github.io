Editor's Desk

The month of April woke up to the rude shock of a big increase in the retail prices of Petroleum Products, with the price at the pump crossing the psychological INR 100 per liter mark. This increase has also resulted in an upward movement in the inflation rate. In addition to this shock, the heat wave has added fuel to the fire and affected the wheat crop. However, the prediction of another normal monsoon has brought some hope for cheer.

India has amendment its Electricity Rules 2020 and plans to eliminate the use of DG sets in coming 5 years to combat air and noise pollution specially in urban areas.

The big positive news of the month has been the signing of India-Australia Economic Cooperation and Trade Agreement. This is the first trade agreement signed by India with a developed economy after more than a decade. .....

Ministry of Commerce & Industry

India-Australia Economic Cooperation and Trade Agreement (IND-AUS ECTA)

Relevant to M&M-IO, Tech M, MHRIL

  • On April 2nd, India and Australia signed a comprehensive interim free-trade agreement that permits zero duty trade on several items.

  • For India, the ECTA with Australia is the first agreement with a large, developed economy of the world after more than a decade. Australia is also the third OECD country after Japan and South Korea with which India has signed a Free Trade Agreement (FTA).

Amendment in Electricity Rules 2020: Use of Diesel Generators

Relevant to Mahindra Powerol

Reserve Bank of India

RBI Caps on Lending Limit of NBFC  

Relevant to MMFSL

  • RBI caps lending limits of NBFCs to bring them on a par with those of banks to close the regulatory gap between bank and NBFCs. It will come into effect from October 1, 2022

  • Over the years, RBI has been harmonising regulations between the NBFCs and the banks, in the wake of the many defaults seen in the shadow banking space in the past.
