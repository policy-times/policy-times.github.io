Ministry of Commerce & Industry

New SEZ Act Proposed: Highlights

Relevant to MLDL


The existing Special Economic Zone (SEZ) law in India is all set to be replaced with a new law. India is proposing to transform its narrow export focused SEZs into comprehensive economic hubs through several concessions and the easing of restrictions to attract more investment in these areas. This is expected to be tabled in the upcoming Monsoon Session of the Parliament once the draft is finalised after stakeholder consultations.

Provisions in the Proposed SEZ Act

  • Industrial units located in these hubs, which will be called Development of Enterprise and Service Hubs (DESH), may be allowed to sell in the domestic market, and contract manufacture for those outside these zones as well.
  • Some fiscal incentives and measures to improve the ease of doing business are also likely to be part of the package to transform SEZs into comprehensive economic zones.
  • These hubs may be established by the Centre or a state or jointly by them, or any person for the manufacture of goods or rendering services or for both.
  • An ‘equalisation levy’ may be imposed on goods or services supplied to the domestic tariff area to bring taxes on them at par with those provided by units outside the zones. Currently, units in SEZs are export focussed and face certain restrictions on sales to domestic market.
  • States looking to set up such zones will be able to set up boards that will be responsible for looking at the SEZs.
  • It is also proposed to relax the mandatory foreign exchange payment for domestic tariff area (DTA) supplies and permit subcontracting both for goods and services for DTA units. The plan is to make the new SEZ scheme compliant with the WTO rules and doing away with the Net Foreign Exchange clause is the first step in that direction.
  • It is proposed to constitute the Integrated Development Hubs Board within fifteen days of the commencement of the act to promote, oversee approvals of, and ensure the development of the development hubs in India.
  • The Integrated Development Hubs Board shall by way of notification, establish such number of State Boards as may be considered necessary to carry out its purposes under this Act.
  • Every such State Board as may be established shall be responsible for the oversight of the Development Hubs located in such States or Union Territories, as may be notified by the Board.
  • There will be Single Window Clearance mechanism for the establishment of Development Hubs.

Comments

Rahul Shukla, Indirect Taxes and SEZ expert Additional fiscal and non-fiscal benefits, including industrial estates being notified as hubs, will hopefully lead to greater benefits.

However, the rules and clarity on the proposed equalisation levy is something trade will be looking at, including greater guidance on the hubs framework.

Conclusion
The proposed new SEZ Law is intended to shift the orientation from export promotion to cluster development promoting world class manufacturing hub or both export and internal consumption. One can hope that this new arrangement would be helpful in getting quality product not only to compete in the external market but also make it available for domestic consumers.
