Editor's Desk
The provisional estimates of the annual national income for 2021–22 and the quarterly estimates of the gross domestic product (GDP) for the fourth quarter released by the National Statistical Office present a bleak picture. Numbers show that the GDP growth in 2021–22 has bounced back to 8.7% after the 6.6% decline in 2020–21. But the quarterly growth rates have steadily declined from 20.1% to 8.4% and then to 5.4% and further to 4.1% over the year. Downward Revision of India’s GDP forecast by almost all the agencies including World Bank and RBI has been one of the highlights of the month. Slump in consumption, surging prices, and slow growth in key sectors are likely to stall the recovery.

The GST Council in its 47th meeting announced changes in GST rates for many of the items which is going to affect the price structure in the economy. The proposed draft SEZ Act will enable the SEZs into Comprehensive Economic Hubs. This would reorient the SEZs for export as well as the domestic markets.

The Government of India approved the draft notification to introduce Bharat NCAP (New Car Assessment Programme). This would provide ratings to new models of cars based on crash test on these models. This is going to change the automobile landscape in India by offering star ratings to help making the consumers informed decision.

The Parliamentary Standing Committee on Commerce released this month a report on the promotion and regulation of e-commerce in India in which the committee expressed the need for a comprehensive National E-commerce Policy to boost the e-commerce ecosystem in India having equal participation of both the domestic and foreign funded e-commerce companies.

RBI announced the blueprint for regulation of payment system in its report, “Payment Vision 2025” in which it proposes a host of innovative payment systems and regulation of BigTechs, Fintechs, buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) systems, and introduction of a Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC).

NITI Aayog’s study on gig economy and recommendations to uplift the conditions of gig workers was another highlight in the policy landscape in India. Categorisation of states by the ministry of commerce based on the implementation of Business Reform Action Plan 2020 is to reflect the record of different states on “ease of doing business” in their respective states.

Other major regulatory developments of the month were the guidelines for misleading advertisements by the Ministry of Consumer Affairs and draft amendments in the IT rules by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology.

On the multilateral front, World Investment Report by UNCTAD and reforms in WTO proposed during its 12th Ministerial Conference were major developments of the month. As per ‘Global Sustainable Development Report 2022’, India’s progress on many of the SDGs are not very encouraging.

Announcement of the maiden budget of Punjab by Aam Admi Party Government and Haryana’s EV (Electric Vehicle) Policy In the beginning of the month and Ground-breaking ceremony to lay foundation of various investment projects in the different parts of Uttar Pradesh were other major developments at the state level.
