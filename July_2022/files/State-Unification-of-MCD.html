Municipal Corporation of Delhi: From Trifurcation to Unification


The Municipal Corporation (Amendment) Act, 2022 comes into effect on May 22, 2022, unifying all the three Municipal Corporations of Delhi. The legislation was approved by Lok Sabha on March 30 and by Rajya Sabha on April 5, 2022. President Ram Nath Kovind gave his assent on April 18, 2022.

Context

 The Municipal Corporation of Delhi was trifurcated into North Delhi Municipal Corporation (NDMC), East Delhi Municipal Corporation (EDMC) and South Delhi Municipal Corporation (SDMC) through the Delhi Municipal Corporation Amendment Act, 1911 (Delhi Act, 12 Of 2011) in 2011 the rule of Sheila Dikshit-led Delhi government with UPA in power at the Centre.

This trifurcation of the corporation was uneven in terms of the territorial divisions and revenue generating potential of each corporation. As a result, there was a huge gap in the resources available to the three corporations, vis-a-vis their obligations.

The gap then widened over a period, increasing the financial difficulties of the three civic bodies, leaving them incapacitated to make timely payment of salaries and retirement benefits to their employees and thereby creating serious impediments in maintaining civic services in Delhi.

Unification

 The MCD is now being unified with the idea to remedy the financial difficulties, unequal distribution of resources, wage-related issues, and frequent strikes by employees.

In exercise of the powers conferred by sub-section (1) of section 3 of the Delhi Municipal Corporation (Amendment) Act, 2022 (10 of 2022), the Central Government constituted the Municipal Corporation of Delhi on the 22nd day of May 2022.

The first step would be to nullify the boundaries of each of the three bodies which would be the easiest task.

The next step would be to bring the employees of the three corporations under one umbrella. While the number of the lower-rank staff will not change much, the numbers of higher-rank posts could decrease by a third.

Implications

  • The first implication will be that current boundaries of civil bodies will end as the entire city except the New Delhi Municipal Corporation (NDMC Area).
  • According to the Delhi Municipal Corporation (Amendment) Act, 2022, the central government will appoint a “special officer" to run the new unified civic body until the first meeting of the corporation is held.
  • There would be a balance of resources, income, and expenses. Currently, North regularly suffers from a delay in the payment of salaries. After the unification, there will be uniformity in payments of salaries.
  • The 2022 Act has introduced unification of three municipal corporation of Delhi (MCDs) on the grounds of equity (tackling uneven municipal revenue), optimal utilisation of resources, transparency, and improved governance with overarching powers to centre to control MCD.
  • Civil planning would improve with a centralised authority for things like parking rates, property tax, and new initiatives for the city.
  • It would be easier for the centre to intervene for bailout if there is a financial crisis. There would be overarching power of the centre on the MCD.
  • Total number of seats would be reduced to 250 from the existing 272.
  • There will be one Mayor and Commissioner in place of three.
  • There will be 25 committees instead of 75.
  • Office of Director Local Bodies will be removed and there will be inclusion of e-governance system for citizen centricity along with efficiency in expenditure.
  • There would be a problem to deal with the surplus employees of the three bodies which would be sizable in number.

Conclusion
Among the governance experts, it has been generally agreed that smaller administrative units make the government more accessible to ordinary people. Economists, too, have found that smaller states have performed better. However, we are witnessing the opposite being proposed by the Delhi Municipal Corporation (Amendment) Bill, 2022, tabled by the union home minister. Such an exceptional proposal, likely to be an act, demands an explanation.

Objective behind unification of MCD after 11 years of its trifurcation is to manage the resources in an efficient way as the three bodies were running into financial stress. Similar arguments of achieving efficiency through decentralisation were given while trifurcation of the MCD 11 years earlier. It needs to be seen how the work efficiency of MCD is improved between centralisation and decentralisation debate as economic motive should out- weight the political one to achieve efficiency.
