Uttar Pradesh

Election Manifesto of Major Political parties in U.P.


 India’s largest state by population, Uttar Pradesh is undergoing State Legislative Assembly election. All the political parties have put their energy to get hold of this important election and released election manifestos. Here is the comparison of election manifestos of the three important parties on important sectors:

Sector Bharatiya Janata Party Samajwadi Party Congress
Agriculture
  • Free electricity for farmers for agriculture in the next five years. ₹25,000 crore ‘Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel Agri-Infrastructure Mission’ to set up cold chain centres, godowns, processing units across the state.
  • Formulation of Minimum Support Price (MSP) for wheat and rice.
  • ₹5000 crore to be spent on modernisation of sugarcane mills. Payment to sugarcane farmers within 14 days and, in case of delay, payment to be done with interest.
  • As many as 6 ‘Mega Food Parks’ to be developed in the state.
  • All farmers to be debt-free by 2025.
  • A debt relief law to be brought in for the benefit of extremely poor farmers.
  • Farmers with less than two acres of land will be provided with two bags of DAP and five bags of urea, electricity for irrigation, interest-free loan, insurance, and pension.
  • Rs 25 lakh to the kin of those farmers who died during the farm protests
  • A farmer's memorial in the memory of farmers who died during farm protests
  • Setting up of Kisan Bazars in all districts.
  • Rural economy and infrastructure to be a priority sector for investment. Minimum of 15 per cent of annual budget of Uttar Pradesh to be allocated for the same.
  • UP Rural Infrastructure Fund to be created for the implementation of Agri and rural development schemes.
  • Farmers/Rural Micro Finance Bank to provide affordable and collateral free loans to landless farmers, small farmers, village artisans and craftsmen.
  • Warehouse receipt System to make warehouse and cold storage receipts a tradable and bankable instrument.
  • Agricultural Debt Relief Act to avoid periodic resort to loan waivers.
  • Strengthening of APMC, development of warehouses and cold storage chains, export-oriented food processing clusters, Potato Export Zone in Kannauj, building of village highways
  • Free electricity for farm irrigation, up to 300 units of electricity free for rural households, Establishment of smart village cluster development authority to dcevelop smart village clusters,
  • Setting up of Rural Agriculture Hubs, and Agri Tech Incubation Centers, measures to promote sustainable agriculture,
  • Setting up of UP Tenant Farmers Empowerment Policy, recognizing woman farmers' right, Framer's fund for natural calamity
  • Setting up of Farmer income and welfare commission to suggest improving the farmers' income on annual basis.
  • Loan waiver to farmers within 10 days if Congress comes to power.
  • A minimum support price (MSP) of ₹2,500 per quintal for wheat and paddy, and ₹400 per quintal price for sugarcane.
  • ₹3,000 compensation will be given to farmers for crop damage by stray cattle.
  • ‘Godhan Nyay Yojna’ will be started under which cow dung will be purchased at ₹2 per kg.
  • Increase in percentage of mixing ethanol in petrol to 15% and later to 20% to increase the income of sugarcane growers
Industry
  • Double the per capita income in the state.
  • Attract investment of approx. INR 10 lakh Crore
  • Place UP number one in Ease of doing business
  • Renew the industrial infrastructure of the state, Atal Industrial Infrastructure Mission to be started.
  • Double the export, employment, and self-employment in 5 years.
  • General Bipin Rawat Defence Industrial Corridor to be established in Bundelkhand.
  • Five world class exhibition and convention centre to be established in the state.
  • Three Data centre parks to be established
  • Youth Start-up mission to be started. Three electronic manufacturing cluster, mega leather park in Kanpur, six industrial parks for MSME, IT parks and Integrated Aqua parks to be established.
  • Industrial Policy 2022 to be brought in to ensure ease of doing business which will also incentivize creation of new jobs.
  • Champion Industry to be set up in every division for uniform growth across state.
  • Land Bank for green field projects to be set up.
  • Time bound single route, single window and under a single roof clearance
  • Industrial area land to be made free hold
  • Land development charges to be waived
  • Rehabilitation of sick units
  • UP State Industrial Security Force to be raised.
  • Electricity rates to be rationalized.
  • High Tech Special Export Zones to be set up along the Agra Lucknow, Bundelkhand and Purvanchal Expressways.
  • Tech Innovation Centres to be set up.
  • Industry to be encouraged to use bio ethanol, environment friendly technology
  • Temporary exemption from certain Labour law Ordinance 2020
  • Union Labour Codes will not be implemented in Uttar Pradesh.
  • Develop Western UP as Urban Mining and Recycling Hub as Meerut already is an automobile recycling centre while Moradabad was recycling 80% of all categories of e-waste.
  • Rs 5 thousand crore seed startup fund for entrepreneurs
Health
  • Establishment of Government medical collages with modern facilities, six Dhanwantari Mega Health Parks, Maharshi Sushrut Health Infrastructure Mission
  • Missin Zero to eliminate vector born diseases, double the ambulance services with modern facilities, Dialysis centre at every district,
  • Double the MBBS seats in the state, employ 6000 doctors and 10,000 para medical staffs, expand the network of Jan Ausadhi Kendras, make U.P. free of TB by 2025.
  • State Health Policy 2022 will be brought in.
  • Cashless health services, policy for setting up of Health cities, Policy for capping price of medical tests, setting up of State Pandemic Relief Agency, upgradation od services at all district hospitals, setting up of 5 regional super specialty hospitals in rural areas, appointments to all vacant posts in state health care system.
  • Expenditure on Health to be 10 per cent of budget expenditure.
  • Increase of 5% in the health department’s budget.
  • Free treatment of up to ₹10 lakh for every patient, who gets admitted to a hospital
Cultural Heritage and Tourism
  • Establishment of cultural centres in Chitrakoot, Varanasi, Shringwerpur; Light and Sound facility at Maharaja Bijli Pasi Fort in Lucknow; Monument in Bahraich; Renovation of Maharshi Valmili Ashram and Sitamarhi Sthal; Ramayan University in Ayodhya; Mega arrangement for Mahakumbh 2025; Surdash Brajbhasa Academy in Mathura; Goswami Tulsidas Awadhi Academy, Keshav Das Bundeli Academi; Sant Kabir Das Bhojpuri Academy; Film City in Noida; and Lata Mangeshkar Performing Arts Academy.
  • Cash incentive and subsidy to promote shootings of Hindi, Bhojpuri and Braj films.
  • Tourism Policy 2022 will be brought in.
  • Tourism recovery package for badly hit tourism sector, one-time financial grant to industry professionals and frontline workers like guides, drivers, tongs operators, Group insurance and pension schemes for them.
  • Tourism budget to be augmented.
  • Organise festival dedicated to Ganga in Prayagraj and Varanasi
Employment
  • One employment and one self-employment in each family in the next 5 years; Free coaching for UPSC, UPPSC, NDA, CDS, JEE, NIIT, TET, CLAT, and other competitive exams
  • Urban Employment Guarantee Act to ensure minimum income to young men and women till they are able to get secure vocation.
  • State Employment Policy to create a detailed roadmap for generating employment.
  • Gap analysis and capacity augmentation, Review of Minimum wages for unskilled and skilled workers, Appointment to all vacant seats in the state healthcare and education system, police force, doubling of paramedical and nursing staff in public health, system, Special drive for recruitment in police force, Smart Kisansahayak scheme to train and employ 5 lakh rural youths for implementing sustainable and environment friendly farming practices.
  • End outsourcing and regularize contractual employees in a phased manner
  • 40% reservation to women in government jobs.
  • Increase in honorarium to ASHA anganwadi workers from existing to INR 10,000 per month.
  • End of outsourcing and regularize contractual employees in a phased manner.
  • Regulaisation of safai workers.
Filling of 2 lakh government teacher vacancies.
