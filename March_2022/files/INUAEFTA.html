Ministry of Commerce and Industry

India UAE Free Trade Agreement: Key Takeaways

 Relevant to M&M - IO

India and UAE signed first Free Trade Agreement
India and UAE signed its first Free Trade Agreement firming up a Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (CEPA). The deal was signed in New Delhi by Commerce and Industry Minister Piyush Goyal and UAE's Minister of Economy Abdulla bin Touq Al Marri.

The Context

  • UAE is the gateway to Africa and the European Union (EU).
  • The UAE is the third-largest trading partner for India in bilateral trade and the second-largest destination for Indian exports after the US
  • The UAE is the eighth-largest investor in India.

Indo-UAE FTA: Key features:

  • It is first bilateral trade agreement in the middle east and North Africa.
  • This FTA aims to expand the bilateral trade to USD 100 billion in the next five years as against USD 43 billion today.
  • Zero duty access to 90 per cent of goods exported from India from the first day of implementation.
  • Investment Zone to be established in India for UAE firms.
  • A dedicated India Mart in Jebel Ali Free Zone
  • Opportunities for Indian investors to be created in advanced industrial technology zones in Abu Dhabi, with focus on logistics, pharma, medical devices, agriculture, steel and aluminum
  • The UAE will support India’s energy needs and ensure affordable supplies.
  • A joint hydrogen task force to be set up to scale up technologies which would boost cooperation in climate actions
  • An IIT to be set up in UAE by India.
  • It has robust rules of origin to protect both economies from misuse of the agreement by third countries
  • The CEPA will be a full agreement which will cover:

    • Trade in Goods
    • Rules of Origin
    • Trade in Services
    • Technical Barriers to Trade
    • Dispute Settlement
    • Sanitary and Phytosanitary Measures
    • Custom Procedures
    • Government Procurement
    • IPR
    • Digital Trade

Negative list:

The following items have been put on negative list which includes areas where manufacturing has been robust and government has rolled out production link incentive schemes:

  • dairy
  • fruit
  • cereals
  • vegetables
  • tea
  • soaps
  • footwear
  • petroleum
  • tyres
  • toys
  • aluminium
  • scrap
  • copper
  • processed marble,
  • coffee
  • tobacco
  • dyes
  • spices
  • sugar
  • tobacco

Benefits to India:

  • It will give zero duty access to 90 per cent of India’s exports to the UAE
  • The agreement would boost the country’s exports in several labour-oriented sectors including gems and jewellery, textiles, leather, footwear, plastics, automobiles, agricultural products, sports goods, engineering goods, and pharmaceuticals and medical devices, furniture, wood products and sports goods.
  • This agreement is going to benefit labor insensitive sectors in India, thus giving boost to employment generation in India.
  • It will provide platforms to SMEs to expand themselves in the international markets.
  • About 26 billion worth of Indian products that are currently subject to 5 per cent import duty to UAE.
  • UAE has committed to make significant investments in India’s infrastructure.
  • In the automobile sector, there is projected increase in exports over 5 years is USD 160 million as average per unit price of Indian manufactured vehicle would decrease by around 5 per cent.
  • Boost to India-UAE bilateral goods trade to USD 100 billion in over 5 years and USD 15 billion for services.
  • Additional exports of agri products projected at USD 850 million in 5 years.

Conclusion:

  • India now prefers country-specific FTAs rather than regional cooperation deals on trade and business.
  • India is looking at reciprocal access, good market conditions and fair play in trade of both goods and services.
  • Apart from UAE, India has commenced talks on FTAs with the UK, the European Union (EU), Australia, Canada and Israel.
  • It will help diversify exports and explore new markets for promotion of its products.
  • This FTA with UAE will give India a foothold in the Arab region which is not only a gateway to Africa but also an important global trade center.
