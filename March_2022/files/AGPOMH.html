Maharashtra

Agriculture Export Policy of Maharashtra
Maharashtra Government has announced its own Agriculture Policy on 25th February, 2022 focusing on export promotion of 21 agricultural commodities.

The Context:
The Government of India had unveiled its Agriculture Export Policy in December 2018, directing the state governments to draft their own policy. Accordingly, the Maharashtra government had constituted a committee in May 2019 to draft the policy.

Objectives

  • To develop entrepreneurs in agri exports
  • To diversifying the export basket to boost high-value and value-added agricultural exports.
  • To provide an institutional mechanism for pursuing market access
  • To tackle barriers and deal with sanitary and phytosanitary issues.

Highlights of the Policy:

  • 21 agricultural commodities have been covered.

    • Banana
    • pomegranate
    • Alphonso Mango
    • Kesar Mango
    • orange, grapes
    • sweet lime
    • onion
    • cashew
    • floriculture
    • raisin
    • vegetables
    • non-Basmati rice
    • pulses and cereals
    • oilseeds
    • jaggery
    • spices (red chilli and turmeric)
    • dairy products
    • fisheries
    • animal products
  • Infrastructure development to promote export.
  • Export of organic products
  • Export of products with a Geographical Indication (GI)
  • Developing sea protocol and trial consignments
  • Hiring international consultants for post-harvest management
  • Declaration of pest-free areas for certain products
  • Implementation of an effective traceability system
  • To encourage farmer producer companies to participate in exports, APEDA, MPEDA, EIC and other commodity boards will provide a framework for ownership of the supply chain, starting from farmer registrations, FPO formation, provision of quality inputs, price discovery, farmer training through technical organisation and third-party certification.
  • Post-harvest losses have been pegged at 5-10% for non-perishables and about 30% for perishables.

Industry’s reactions:

Sunil Pawar, Managing Director of the Maharashtra State Agriculture Marketing Board
  • The state has received the GI tag for 26 agricultural products and the export policy will focus on boosting exports of these products
Anoop Kumar, Chief Secretary (Cooperation and Marketing), Maharashtra
  • The policy focuses on exports which have the potential of increasing the net income of farmers by about 40-45 percent

Conclusion:
As domestic market has limited capacity to give remunerative returns to farmers, this agri export policy has the potential to increase export of agri commodities from Maharashtra resulting into the growth in farmers’ income. This export policy is going to give policy stability which is very important for sustained growth in exports and gain customer’s trustworthiness.
