Editor's Desk
The Budget for 2022-23 was presented in February. Unsurprisingly, there has been a divergence of opinion on the takeaways. The Government projected an economic growth of 11.1 per cent (which has since been revised downwards) for the next year. The Finance Minister is holding wide post-budget consultations with various stakeholders. The focus on assesses and removing roadblocks has been a key focus. The Government wants to be viewed as assesse friendly.

Trade Deals are back on the table. We signed the India-United Arab Emirates (UAE) Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (CEPA) earlier this month. This was done in double quick time. The deal was concluded despite the recent push on “ Atmanirbhar Bharat” and strong encouragement to the domestic industry. This Trade deal has sent a positive signal to Countries that have complementarities with us. This FTA can also be a game changer as it not only includes trade in goods and services but also encourages investment in Government procurement.

The ensuing State Assembly Elections across five States is being viewed as a ‘semi- final” to the 2024 National Elections. Over the last few weeks, all major political parties have been subsumed by these elections. We have reviewed the election manifestos of major political parties contesting in the politically important UP to understand the possible policy direction post the election results.

Introduction of the Green Hydrogen Policy is an important step in the direction of sustainable development. Further, release of the draft ‘India Data Accessibility and Use’ policy by MeitY for public consultation is another major development in the policy arena. The Rajasthan government’s budget which was announced a few days ago included implementation of old pension scheme among others. A separate Agriculture Budget was presented in the Rajasthan Assembly for the first time.

The Russia-Ukraine Crisis is creating a significant impact and has economic implications for India. The next few months will be interesting.
