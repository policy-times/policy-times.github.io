Rajasthan Highlights

Rajasthan Budget 2022-23
On 23rd February 2022, Budget of 2022-23 was presented by the Chief Minister of Rajasthan, Mr. Ashok Gehlot in the Rajasthan State Legislative assembly. Focus of this budget was Health, education, infrastructure, social security, and tourism.

Highlights of Rajasthan Budget of 2022

Pension Scheme
  • Old pension scheme for employees appointed on and after January 1, 2014
Agriculture
  • First Separate Agriculture Budget
  • Allocation of Rs 5,000 crore for CM Krishak Saathi Yojna, which was Rs 2,000 crore in the last Budget
  • Rs 2,700 crore for the Rajasthan Micro Irrigation Mission, under which 5 lakh farmers will be benefitted
  • 125 days of employment in MGNREGA instead of 100 days. The state government will bear the expenditure of Rs 700 crore
  • 11 mini food park, mini agro park at Chainpura (Niwai), Tonk
  • Phyto sanitary labs in Kota and Jodhpur
  • 18 new agricultural collages
Health
  • Increase the health cover under the state government's much ambitious Chiranjeevi Health Insurance Scheme from Rs 5 lakh to Rs 10 lakh per year per family. He also announced that IPD and OPD services will be free-of-cost in state-run hospitals.
Electricity
  • 50 units free electricity for those using 100 units, Rs 3/per unit grant for all domestic consumers up to 150 units and Rs 2/unit grant for consumers of 150 to 300 units and above as per the slab. The state government will spend Rs 4,500 crore
Education
  • 1,000 English medium schools to be opened in both urban and rural areas
  • 10,000 English medium teachers to be recruited.
Employment
  • Recruitment of about 1 lakh additional posts in various departments in the coming year
Tourism and Hospitality o Industry Status to Tourism and Hospitality
  • Tourism Development Fund of Rs 1000 crore
  • Adventure Tourism promotion scheme
  • Recruitment of 500 “Paryatak Mitra” for tourist places.
  • Reimbirshment of SGST for hotel and tour operators up to 50 per cent for the period- January 2022-March 2022
  • Rates of land for resorts, sport resorts, health resort, camping site, amusement park, animal safari park will be determined equal to agriculture rates, convention centre/community hall will be equal to residential rates in urban areas and agriculture rate in rural areas.
Transport
  • Exemption of 50 per cent Motor Vehicle Tax on CNG Kit Reinforcement Vehicle.
  • Exemption Divisional Contract Carriage Permits be allowed with 70 per cent rebate on Motor Vehicle Tax charged from all Rajasthan Permits.
Road and planned development
  • Major repair works, work on missing link roads
  • 1000 km state highways to be converted to two lanes
  • Rajasthan Smart City Scheme for integrated development of Jodgpur, Bikaner, Bharatpur, Alwar, Bhilwara, and Chittorgarh
  • Extension of Metro-Badai Choppar to Transport Nager and Mansarovar to 200 feet bypass Ajmer Road
  • Mukhya Mantri Regional Rural Development Scheme for infrastructure and rural development in remote and backward areas of the state. Constitution of Development Authorities of Udaipur and Kota.
Industries
  • Exemption of e-way bill for transportation of goods within the city extended from Rs 1 lakh to Rs 2 lakh
  • The period for exemption for MSMEs from sanction and inspection extended from 3 to 5 years
  • Exemption from conversion charges for converting private agriculture land for white category industry/enterprises purposes in rural areas.
  • Upper limit of stamp duty reduced to Rs 50 crore on instruments of merger/demerger of companies
  • In RIICO areas for specified industries plots will be allotted directly along with auction process.
  • New Rajasthan Investment Promotion Scheme (RIPS)-2022 will be introduced with better benefits to enterprises.
